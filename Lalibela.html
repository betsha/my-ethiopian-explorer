Lalibela

Lalibela is one of Ethiopia’s most remarkable sites, known for its rock-hewn churches carved directly from stone in the 12th century.
King Lalibela built these eleven monolithic churches to serve as a “New Jerusalem” for Christians who could not make the pilgrimage to the Holy Land.

The churches are still in use today and connected through narrow tunnels and passageways. Each church has unique architectural features and spiritual importance.
Lalibela is a sacred site for Orthodox Christians and an architectural wonder that attracts visitors from around the world.
