Gondar

Gondar, located in the highlands of northern Ethiopia, was once the capital of the Ethiopian Empire.
It is best known for Fasil Ghebbi, a royal enclosure containing a complex of castles and palaces built by Emperor Fasilides in the 17th century.

Often referred to as the “Camelot of Africa,” Gondar is rich in imperial architecture and Ethiopian Orthodox churches with colorful murals.
The city is also famous for the annual Timket (Epiphany) celebration, one of Ethiopia’s most important religious festivals.
