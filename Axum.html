Aksum

Aksum, located in northern Ethiopia, is a city of immense historical significance. It served as the capital of the Aksumite Empire, one of the greatest civilizations of the ancient world.
Aksum is famous for its towering obelisks (stelae), believed to be royal tombs, and for being the legendary home of the Queen of Sheba.

The city is also said to be the final resting place of the biblical Ark of the Covenant, housed in the Church of St. Mary of Zion. Aksum was once a major trade center, connecting Africa with Asia and Europe.
It remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a pilgrimage site for Ethiopian Orthodox Christians.
