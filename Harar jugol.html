Harar Jugol

Harar, known as the "City of Saints," is a vibrant and colorful city in eastern Ethiopia.
It is considered the fourth holiest city in Islam and is home to over 80 mosques and 100 shrines. The old walled city, known as Harar Jugol, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Harar is also famous for its lively markets, narrow alleys, and traditional homes with richly decorated interiors.
One of the city’s unique traditions is the nightly feeding of hyenas, which has been practiced for generations. Harar blends Islamic culture, African traditions, and unique architecture into one unforgettable experience.
